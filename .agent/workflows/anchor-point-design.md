## Brand & Style

This design system is engineered for the next generation of Sailors, balancing the gravitas of military tradition with the high-octane energy of modern athletic brands. The aesthetic is **High-Contrast / Bold**, utilizing massive typography and a rigid structural grid to convey a sense of order and institutional strength. 

The experience is designed to feel "mission-ready." It avoids decorative fluff in favor of clean lines, purposeful whitespace, and high-impact photography that places the user directly into the action. It is professional, patriotic, and built for speed, ensuring that critical HR information is delivered with the same precision as a flight deck operation.

## Colors

The palette is anchored in Navy Blue and Athletic Gold, creating a high-visibility interface that demands attention. 

- **Primary (Deep Navy):** Used for the core background and structural elements to maintain a professional, authoritative base.
- **Secondary (Athletic Gold):** Reserved for high-priority calls to action, key data highlights, and status indicators. It represents energy and excellence.
- **Stark White:** Used for primary text and iconography on dark backgrounds to ensure maximum readability and a clean, modern finish.
- **The Palette Strategy:** The default mode is dark to reduce eye strain and provide a sophisticated, technical feel. Use heavy saturation for action states and muted tones of Navy for secondary containers.

## Typography

The typography uses **Lexend** across all levels to provide a geometric, athletic, and highly readable experience. 

Headlines must be set in uppercase with heavy weights (700+) to evoke military stencil aesthetics without the cliché. Large display type should be tightly tracked to create "blocks" of information that feel solid and immovable. Body copy maintains generous line heights to ensure clarity during mobile use in high-stress or fast-moving environments.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system employs a **Fluid Grid** based on a 4-column mobile architecture. The spacing rhythm is strictly mathematical, utilizing a 4px base unit to ensure alignment across all components.

Margins are set wide (20px) to frame content and prevent the UI from feeling cluttered. Content blocks should be separated by aggressive vertical stacks (48px) to create clear section breaks, mirroring the bold headers found in military briefings. Photography should frequently bleed to the edges of the screen to increase immersion.

## Elevation & Depth

Hierarchy is established through **Bold Borders** and **Tonal Layers** rather than soft shadows. This creates a "hard-surface" aesthetic that feels durable and technical.

- **Level 0 (Base):** Deep Navy background.
- **Level 1 (Surface):** A slightly lighter tint of Navy with a 1px solid stroke in Athletic Gold or White (10% opacity) to define edges.
- **Level 2 (Inlay):** High-contrast Gold surfaces for active states.
- **Shadows:** Avoid diffused shadows. If depth is required, use a "Hard Shadow" (100% opacity, 2px offset) to create a brutalist, stacked effect that feels architectural.

## Shapes

The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px roundedness)**. Every button, card, and input field features 90-degree angles to reinforce the professional, disciplined nature of the Navy. 

The only exception to this rule is for purely circular elements, such as user avatars or specific status pips. The use of hard corners allows components to lock together perfectly, creating a sense of a "modular system" where every piece has a designated, secure place.

> **Exceptions:** The `ExpandableBottomDrawer` (Bottom Pill Bar) is explicitly exempt from the 0px roundedness rule. When in its floating "pill" state (State 0), it must maintain fully rounded edges (e.g., 40px radius) and horizontal margins (e.g., 20px) to preserve its distinct tactile feel, morphing to sharp corners only when expanded.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid Athletic Gold with Black text. They occupy the full width of the content area to maximize touch target and visual impact. Secondary buttons use a thick 2px White border with no fill.
- **Cards:** Cards use a Level 1 Surface (tinted Navy) with a heavy top-border (4px) in Gold to signal importance. Photography within cards should use a subtle dark-to-transparent gradient overlay to ensure white text remains legible.
- **Chips/Status Tags:** Rectangular blocks with high-contrast fills. For "Released" or "Active" states, use Gold background. For "Pending," use a simple White outline.
- **Input Fields:** Bottom-border only or fully enclosed sharp boxes. When focused, the border weight increases from 1px to 3px in Gold.
- **Progress Bars:** Use thick, blocky segments rather than a smooth continuous line to represent milestones, giving a tactical "loading" feel.
- **Imagery:** Use high-grain, high-contrast photography with cool blue color grading to match the primary palette.
